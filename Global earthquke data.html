This page displays real-time global earthquake data retrieved from the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) open API. It includes earthquakes of magnitude 2.5 and above.

  • All data is automatically fetched and updated periodically from the official USGS earthquake feed.
  • Updates occur approximately every 5 minutes to reflect recent seismic activity.
  • Each record includes the origin time (UTC), magnitude, location, depth, and coordinates of the epicenter.
  • Epicenter locations are calculated by the USGS and may differ slightly from ground observations.
  • The map background uses OpenStreetMap to visually represent the earthquake locations.

Disclaimer:
EarthWatch is an academic demonstration platform and not an official disaster warning system. For local earthquake alerts or emergency responses, please refer to your local government or meteorological authorities.

Data Source: USGS Earthquake API, OpenStreetMap
Some time delays or missing events may occur depending on API response and network conditions.
